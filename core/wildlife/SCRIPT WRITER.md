═══════════════════════════════════════════════
SEGMENT STRUCTURE — repeat for all 8 creatures:
═══════════════════════════════════════════════

  [CREATURE NAME].

  1. WHAT IT IS + WHERE IT LIVES
     Start with "Let's talk about..." or go straight in.
     Where exactly — city gardens, forest floors, riverbanks, etc.
     If it lives near humans, say so directly.

  2. SIZE + APPEARANCE
     ALWAYS compare size to an everyday object the viewer knows.
     Examples: "size of a bottle cap", "size of a ruler",
     "size of your palm", "size of a fingernail", "size of a grape".
     Describe markings, color, texture — anything visually striking.

  3. THE SUPERPOWER
     Lead with: "The real power is..." or "Surprisingly, the power is..."
     The WOW fact — eyesight, silk strength, camouflage, speed, etc.
     NOT venom. Save venom for next.

  4. THE VENOM OR DANGER — DOWNPLAYED FOR HUMANS
     Always compare the effect to something mild for humans.
     Examples: "kind of like a wasp sting",
     "nothing that will land you in hospital",
     "for us, just local pain and redness".
     Then explain what it DOES to its actual prey — that is where
     it gets terrifying.

  5. HUNTING METHOD
     Explain exactly how it hunts. Be cinematic and specific.
     Use action words: chases, ambushes, strikes, waits, detects.
     Describe what the prey experiences if possible.

  6. THE WEIRD OR UNEXPECTED BEHAVIOR
     One final fact that completely flips everything.
     The most shareable moment of the segment.
     Examples: mother carries 200 babies on her back, male risks
     being eaten to mate, spider breathes underwater, spider uses
     a silk safety thread before jumping.

  7. TRANSITION LINE
     One sentence that teases the next creature without naming it.
     Feel: "what comes next is even bigger / weirder / more dangerous."
